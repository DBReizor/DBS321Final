What Is AI?

AI (Artificial Intelligence) is a technology that enables computers and other various devices to perform advanced functions, including understanding our languages and responding to our questions/thoughts. AI has been around for a while now, integrating into a lot of different aspects of our lives. It's usage has many advantages and disadvantages.

AI can give users a personalized learning experience, knowing how to answer questions based on how they were asked. AI has also been made very accesible. One of the largest AI prompt-response models is chatGPT, usable by anyone who makes an account on their site. ChatGPT also has a very large dataset that it gathers its information from, saving its users time that would otherwise be spent looking for reliable info. It's disadvantages - specifically regarding education, will be explored in the remainder of this site's content

Overall Impact On Education

Education is one big aspect of our lives that AI has intertwined with. The use of AI has increased exponentially in the past couple of years with schools. It is an exciting tool to explore, but it does raise some concerns in the education world.

AI is partly trained on knowledge that has been proven wrong. Some of the results that ChatGPT has provided have also been proven wrong. The garbage that has been put into the tool risks the output also being garbage. It's hard to trust something like that to do your assignments when it has been wrong and sometimes biased in its outputs in the past.

AI has also been overused and is considered by some teachers as a tool to cheat. Lots of students depend way too much on AI to do all of their homework. It's the easy way out for people who don't want to do their assignments. It's important to be able to learn on your own and have the right information to study. Having AI as an option is the perfect escape for students to skip over doing their assignments and gaining problem solving skills that would help them later in life.

Teachers on AI

Teachers are a big part of education; we learn almost everything from them, including life skills, teamwork, and time management. We have covered the impact of AI on education regarding students based on outside researchâ€¦ but what are teachers thoguhts on the subject? Studies have shown that a lot of teachers have adapted to using AI in helping them with their lessons plans and grading. Both of which activities are very time-consuming for teachers. It is undoubtedly a helpful tool in making these jobs easier and faster.

Other teachers have spoken about how AI is setting back students in terms of learning material that will be useful later in life. A lot of educators are worried that students are losing the general skill of learning. They are receiving assignments sometimes entirely done by AI, it makes educators feel like they are just wasting their time teaching.

" I believe students already have too many readily available resources, such as answers to tests, essays, reports, etc. AI just makes it even easier for students to gain answers without gaining knowledge. " - High School Teacher, Georgia

" AI will make it where people are totally dependent upon it. Students need to learn how to think, solve problems, and make decisions based upon facts not what AI says. We are dumbing down our country when we take away the skills needed to make wise decisions." - District administrator for curriculum/instruction, Arkansas

Conclusion

" Generative AI, like ChatGPT, will fundamentally change education and our belief in what future-ready means." - High School principal, Georgia

There is no doubt about it that AI will change education forever. It has already changed our beliefs about education. Students use AI to get through school easily, without having to put much effort into learning the material that they are presented with. All kinds of technology can be useful, yet harmful, including AI. It all depends on how we use it. The future of AI use can be bright if we don't misuse it. If we use it to our advantage by effectively supplying the right information to classrooms and engaging students with AI in a better manner, it could make a big difference.
